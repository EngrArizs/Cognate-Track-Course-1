This circuit project uses an Arduino Uno to control multiple LEDs connected through digital output pins. Each LED is paired with a resistor to limit the electrical current and protect the components from damage. The breadboard is used to organize the circuit connections and provide a common ground connection for all LEDs.

The Arduino sends signals to the LEDs, allowing them to turn on and off in different sequences or patterns depending on the uploaded program code. The jumper wires connect the Arduino pins to the LEDs and breadboard, making communication between components possible.

This setup demonstrates the basic principles of electronics and programming, including digital output control, current flow, circuit wiring, and LED sequencing. It is commonly used in beginner Arduino activities to help students understand how hardware and software work together in creating interactive electronic systems.
